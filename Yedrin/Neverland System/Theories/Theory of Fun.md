The **Theory of Fun** says that if something isn't fun or at least *interesting*, the [[Tables|Table]] should look at ways to *make* it fun. There's a common adage that everything is more *interesting* with explosions. Long biology lectures might not be *interesting* to the Table, but if ninjas with guns interrupt the lecture and turn it into a hostage situation, [[Players]] now have the opportunity to turn a learning moment into an action scene. Alternatively, the Table might find a horde of sword-wielding Samurai boring after weeks of slogging through the [[Vulcan Constellation]]; maybe turning *this* fight into a lecture on [[Vulcanites|Vulcanite]] biology and culture might be more engaging!

These [[Theories]] are *to taste*, since too much of one [[Elements|Element]] or another risks causing different Players at the Table [[Discomfort|discomfort]]. Understanding the other Players is a soft skill that may take years to fully develop, but keeping an open line of communication is important; Players should let the [[Directors]] know when they're not having fun, and the Directors should frequently ask Players about their favorite moments so far. [[Intermissions|Downtimes]] are great for checking in on Players, since there's no risk of [[Conflicts|Conflict]] during Downtime.

Players should not feel afraid to ask the Directors to make a [[Scenes|Scene]] more interesting. These [[Theories]] could be used mechanically to ask the Directors [[to enjoin]] additional [[Actors]] to a [[Conflicts|Conflict]] or [[to stake]] the stakes of the Scene; if the story isn't interesting enough, it probably needs the additional flavor.

Just remember: these Theories are to taste! Make sure the whole Table [[Discomfort|consents]] before invoking it. Players might be having fun without the additional spice.
