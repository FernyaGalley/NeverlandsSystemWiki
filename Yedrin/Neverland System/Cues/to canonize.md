*Tenses (v): canonized (past), canonize (present), canonizing (progressive)*
*Laconic: To move a Statement from the Rack to Canon.*

To **canonize** a [[Statements|Statement]] is to play it to [[Canons|Canon]] and remove it from the [[Racks|Rack]]. In order to be canonized, a Statement must first exist on the Rack. To start the process of canonizing, [[Directors]] may [[to close|close]] the [[Conflict State Handbook|Conflict State]] and ask for a response from the [[Tables|Table]]. If no Players [[to respond|respond]] with a [[Appropriateness and Relevance|relevant]] Statement that [[to challenge|challenges]] the Statement being canonized within a [[response window|reasonable time-frame]], then the Statement is removed from the Rack and played to Canon.
## Feature - (Canonized)
*see also [[to establish|(Established)]] (synonym) and [[to dispute|(Disputed)]] (antonym)*

[[Statements]] are [[to imply|implied]] to have this [[Features|Feature]] [[to introduce|introduced]] at the precise moment they are moved into [[Canons|Canon]]. (Canonized) Statements as a rule behave more like [[Facts]] than [[Fictions]]. They may still be treated as [[Opinions]] or [[Alternatives]], but must also be considered as [[to validate|valid]] interpretations of reality.
