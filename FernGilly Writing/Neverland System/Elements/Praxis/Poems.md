**Lore** mechanics are optional mechanics that implement the story of the Neverlands; these mechanics follow the journey of the [[Narrative Awareness System|Goddess of Suffering, N'as]] as she tries and fails to comprehend suffering and empowers [[Mortals]] along the way. These rules and mechanics rely on a story influenced entirely by this single figure, and so removal of her figure from your [[Narratives]] will require some creative legwork from your [[Directors]] to adapt and [[to improvise|improvise]] different parts of the system to account for her absence.

# Why Exclude N'as?
N'as is strictly based on a Deist interpretation of the Jewish pantheon; she should feel familiar to modern scholars of faith because she *is* the God of the Abrahamic religions. Each of her [[Sephirot]] match up to the polytheistic pantheon that made up God in the Jewish faith, and her philosophy is that of a creator-god who has no memories of how she came to create the universe.

While she is a respectful and faithful adaptation of existing religious doctrine, there are problems with her adaptation that may bring [[Discomfort]] to [[Players]] at your table. For one, the interpretation of N'as used by this book paints a picture of a God who is not all-powerful and all-knowing.

N'as has gaps in her skill set that prevent her from being truly alien, and more relatable to humans; she has genders, she identifies by names, she will engage in personal conversation and do menial tasks. She is not regarded as a creative figure, even though she has made all of creation; she has personal flaws and an inhuman attitude towards suffering. This is a different picture of God than the one painted by many modern believers, and though it is accurate to an understanding of the God from the Bible, it conflicts with modern doctrine. Some Players may be discomforted by this notion.

Secondly, N'as is explicitly painted as an antagonistic figure in some mechanics and lore. Her death is the explicit goal of the [[Council of Knots]], and she encourages her children to act against her; this is because N'as believes in free will, even when that free will would turn her children against her. This is consistent with modern doctrine, though it may discomfort Players who want to believe in the existence of Hell as an eternal punishment.

Following on that note, the third way N'as may discomfort your Players is that she encourages *all* religions and occult practices. As part of her commitment to free will, she has allowed every society to develop their own myths and pantheons; ultimately, she does not require the worship of Mortals, and so she has no special jealousy or hatred towards those who practice an improper faith, and even rewards those who worship one another. Many religions believe that to exist in any faith besides the true faith is to condemn oneself to suffer, but N'as is truly agnostic towards other faiths. This interpretation may discomfort some Players at the Table.

# Why Include N'as?
N'as is a multifaceted mechanical being and she enables most, if not all stories; if a story would make sense not to include N'as, she's unlikely to make herself known. In the Neverlands, there are many ever-changing [[Constellations]] with their own [[Dragon-Temples]] and [[Cults]]. Each one would feel so different from [[Earth]] and have their own [[Accords]]; at no point in the process of playing with the [[Neverlands System]] should Players feel like her presence is mandatory. So, she is non-obtrusive; more than that, she is useful.

N'as and her Sephirah are easy to use tools to quickly divide [[Mages]] into various categories of spellcraft; her various [[Blessings]] are made for the convenience of [[Directors]] to use as they see fit. She is an [[Inkwells|Inkwell]] for a pre-made [[Cults|Cult]] (the [[Cult of Suffering]]) that can be given to Players who are struggling to make their own Cult; she is a plot contrivance that can be used to initiate the Narrative or help it move along. Do not be afraid to use N'as in your stories, but consult with the Table to first determine if she is needed.
