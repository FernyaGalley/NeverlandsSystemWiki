*Tenses (v): melted (past), melt (present), melting (progressive)*
*Laconic: to simplify a concept* 

To **melt** a [[Statements|Statement]] is to reduce it into another form. Typically, melting will create an [[Alternatives|Alternative]] and play it to the [[Racks]] during an [[Conflicts|Open Conflict State]]; the Player is presenting an alternative hypothesis for how an event occurred. Melted Statements are usually *simplified*, lacking [[Threads]] to Alternative and relying purely on [[Lines]].

Ultimately, the [[Players|Player]] who made the targeted Statement has final say as to whether or not a Melted Statement is [[to accept|accepted]] into [[Canons|Canon]], so long as their objection is based on the rules of [[Discomfort]]. If there is no Discomfort, the targeted Player defers judgement to the [[Directors|Director]]; 

## Feature - (Melted)
*see also [[to wick|(Wicked)]] (antonym), as well as its corollaries [[to snuff|(Snuffed)]] and [[to burn|(Burned)]]*

Whenever a [[Statements|Statement]] is melted, it is [[to introduce|introduced]] with (Melted); this [[Features|Feature]] ought to be [[to denote|denoted]] when introduced, as it signals there is a linked [[Alternatives|Alternative]] to this Feature in [[Canons|Canon]] or on the [[Racks|Rack]].  (Melted) Statements are Alternatives in addition to their other [[Major Dynamics]]; they represent a possible interpretation of the same events. When the Statement referenced by a (Melted) Alternative is removed from the Rack, the [[Directors]] should [[to close|close]] the [[Conflict State Handbook|Conflict State]] and immediately [[to resolve|resolve]] the (Melted) Alternative.
