The [[Threads]] that bind cause and effect are not always clear.

From a certain perspective, at all times each and every atom in our body is pulled or pushed by its electrons against every particle of air and surface it comes into contact with; we could say that by seating ourselves against a chair, a chair was having some impact upon our mood. Could it be, then, that by pressing a cup against your chair, and then against your skin, would it be different from pressing a cup directly against your skin? Would we consider that by two objects coming into contact with one another they have been 'changed' in some way, the same way mixing flour and water can create dough and change the physical properties of the water?

Magical thinking is the attribution of cause and effect to more than that which we can see, extending into extrasensory causes; this cup and *that* cup are different, because one is clearly possessed of magic and has magical influences upon us. *That* sock was worn when an [[Actors|Actor]] *[[to witness|witnessed]]* their team winning the Superbowl, so *that* sock is the *lucky* sock. That [[Actors|Actor]] was present when the farm went through a terrible blight, so she *must* be a [[Witch|witch]].

Superstition is a powerful thing; if an Actor can identify that your opponent believes in superstition, then that Actor could use their belief their [[Opinions]] are [[to validate|valid]] as proof that *other* Opinions are valid; that their use of a magic [[Inkwells|Inkwell]] means *other* Actors can dip into *their* magic Inkwell.

The Rule of Magical Thinking applies whenever an Actor uses magic, spells or believes in a superstition, Actors may posit that to disprove *their* belief, they must discard their own belief in magical thoughts. Forcing someone into a non-magical [[Conflicts|Conflict]] is [[Risk and Duress|risky]] and dangerous, so all [[to enjoin|enjoined]] parties to a Conflict must first agree that they are willing [[to melt]] all their [[to cast|(Cast)]] Statements before they can use this Rule to disable all magic in the Conflict.
