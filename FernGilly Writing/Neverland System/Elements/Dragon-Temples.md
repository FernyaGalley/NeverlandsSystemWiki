A **Dragon-Temple** is a [[Stages|Stage]] and an [[Actors|Actor]], a [[Courts|Court]] and a [[Divines|Divine]]. It is all of these things, because a Dragon-Temple is a city, a settlement, a region of the world; it is the local land god and it is the people that tend to it. Dragon-Temples can be seen as great concepts [[Cults]] are built around; a Dragon-Temple is usually founded or named by someone considered to be important at that time, then over the generations becomes a well-known feature of that [[Constellations|Constellation]].

Settlements and [[Sanctuaries]] are almost all Dragon-Temples; they have a name, [[Mortals]] live inside them and people consider themselves to be part of that Dragon-Temple. A Chicagoan lives in Chicago, and Chicago is a living, breathing thing; a squirrel considers itself to belong to the forest it was born in, and the fish considers every river and sea its home. Even an office worker may consider their workplace to be a Dragon-Temple! Each of these places is represented by a Dragon-Temple of varying strength and power.

Dragon-Temples are only eliminated when the last drop of water is removed from their river, leaving the skeleton of a creek behind. Dragon-Temples may have their boundaries shift, or bend, or even break in times of war, but they will always adapt to their new shapes for as long as people believe in their existence. Dragon-Temples often bend with the river, they do not fight the way the channels twist and turn.

A Dragon-Temple has four components: an [[Accords]], the [[Dragon Breath]], the [[Dragon Scales]] and the [[Dragon Heart]]. The Accords define the physics and cultural expectations of a Dragon-Temple; the Breaths are all the things inside a Dragon-Temple that help them support life. The Scales of a Dragon-Temple are their boundaries and walls which protect the life within, and the Hearts of a Dragon-Temple update Accords and manage the Mortals within. Hearts are staffed by [[Priestesses]], [[Mages]] and [[Divines]] who have vowed to serve the Dragon-Temple to the best of their ability.

[[to win|To win]] a game of the [[Neverlands System]], the [[Tables|Table]] must create and nurture a [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] to maturity. One of the [[Actors]] of the [[Players]] may have to become a [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] and play out their [[Cues]]; the [[Directors]] are welcome to play this role instead if they see it would bring the table [[Discomfort|discomfort]].
