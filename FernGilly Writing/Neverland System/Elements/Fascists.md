*Laconic: A morally degenerated creature, typically a Mortal.*

A **Fascist** is the result of moral decay over an extended period of time. As an Actor thinks less of other Actors and treats them like mere Props to be used and discarded, they commit a cardinal sin. The Fascist will soon forget those feelings they once cherished; compassion, imagination, reason and joy. They reduce the people around them to mere categories, boxes they can fit all their negative preconceptions inside.

There is no kindness hidden deep within the Fascist, and they believe in nothing; they have become devils, great works of evil corrupted by their own hatred. They imitate human speech, pretending to be cognizant of the world, but are dead and hollow on the inside. To rescue a Fascist is to free them from their own ideology; they cling so strongly to their worldview that in some moments, the only form of freedom they can experience is death.

Fascists are not to be taken lightly. They, like any other Actor, are free to make decisions. Free will can be dangerous in their hands; Fascists spread their ideology to unwary victims, poisoning them with a toxin that eats away at their soul. The weak succumb; they become (Craven), then (Belligerent). Their souls are chipped away, until they become (Depraved) or worse... (Dreadful). The strong undo the shackles the words of the Fascist place on their heart; they resist that primal urge to succumb to the strong, to kill and to take what is not theirs. The lucky few develop a moral backbone, an immunity to the Fascist curse and a true immunity to the lies they spread.

Fascists have lost their names to their dark affliction. They are known simply by their title and a letter; a Fascist doctor might be known by **Dr. Q**, where a Fascist fae might be known as **L the Conqueror**. Only by freeing themselves of the shackles of Fascism can a Fascist reclaim their name.

Players are prohibited from playing Fascists. Actors in play may be *former* Fascists, but they must not carry the disease of Fascism, as becoming a Fascist is one of few loss conditions in the game. An Actor cannot succumb to Fascism unless their Player is willing to cease playing them until their Fascism is cured.

Directors may play Fascists, but only in the abstract; a Fascist is an obstacle to be employed only as an opposing narrative force to the Players. Players may attempt to make deals with Fascists; Fascists will always break their word at their soonest convenience. Players may attempt to reform Fascists; one look away and the Fascist may vanish, to return to their people. Players may attempt to confront Fascists directly... but the Fascist is willing to kill and only looking for an opportunity to do so without reprisal.

## Types of Fascists

#### (Militarized) Fascists
(Militarized) Fascists are almost impossible to overwhelm with Strikes, Shots and Spells. (Militarized) Fascists Snuff all Cues which target them, should they outnumber the Players twenty to one. It does not matter if they are grouped up or spread out throughout a building; a (Militarized) Fascist compound cannot be taken down directly.

That does not mean there are no means to take down Fascists; Fascists traveling in patrols are possible to successfully ambush, even if they wield dangerous weaponry. Siege tactics show some effectiveness, as would leading a force large enough to even out the (Militarized) Fascist's numerical advantages; they are only immune to targeted Cues if they outnumber their foes twenty men to one divine. Twenty men to one divine and a *dog?* They may quake in their boots.
#### (Figurehead) Fascists
The exception to the rule, (Figurehead) Fascists may be known by their full name and their title, eg. President Armand. Figurehead Fascists represent the final obstacle Players must overcome in order [[to win]]. Figurehead Fascists can seamlessly employ the abilities of all other Fascists, as well as the magical abilities of Mages. They have strength rivaling the Divines; simply ambushing a (Figurehead) Fascist will not be enough to defeat them. (Figurehead) Fascists are ultimately replaceable; if they are assassinated in the quiet of night, a new (Figurehead) Fascist will rise to replace them. To win, their ideology must be well and truly refuted in an open forum, visible to the public and unequivocally a victory. The exact details of how the final Conflict might play out are provided on the [[Dances]] page.
