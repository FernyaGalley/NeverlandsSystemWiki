## Lore

To **target** an [[Game Mechanics|Element]] is 

Determining what Targets a Cue actually targets will require critical thinking skills [[Rule of Outside Scope|outside the scope of this work]]. Listen to the [[Players|Player]] [[to cast|casting]] the Cue and determine how the Cue actually plays out before determining what they *intended* to target; afterwards, make the determination of what they *actually* targeted at point of Cue resolution.

For example, a Cue might be aimed at an [[Actors|Actor]], only for that Actor to duck down and tie their shoes; the Cue might actually splash harmlessly against a wall. On the other hand, a Cue intended [[to hurt]] the feelings of an Actor might be audible to a guilty eavesdropper; if they can [[to observe|observe]], they may also be a valid target of the Cue! Use your best judgement and talk it over with the [[Tables|Table]] when in doubt.

## Feature - (That) and (Those)

When an [[Game Mechanics|Element]] has been [[to inflict|inflicted]] with **(That)** or **(Those)**, it is the [[Targets|Target]] of the current [[Cues|Cue]]. When the Cue [[to resolve|resolves]], it will [[to manipulate|manipulate]] those Targets! Additionally, if the Cue has a [[Features|Feature]] (such as [[to target|(That)]] or [[to target|(Those)]] for [[to target]]), (Those) Targets will be inflicted with (That) Feature.

[[Directors]] should take note that both (That) and (Those) are *[[to imply|(Implied)]]* Features, and will not be necessary [[to denote]].
## Mechanics of (That) and (Those)

**Mechanically,** [[to target|(That)]] and [[to target|(Those)]] both [[to denote|denote]] the current [[Targets]] of a [[Cues|Cue]]. They are [[to imply|(Implied)]] [[Features]] and typically do not need to be denoted.
