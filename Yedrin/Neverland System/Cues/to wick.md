*Tenses (v): wicked (past), wick (present), wicking (progressive)*
*Laconic: To make something seem more magic than it is.*

To **wick** a [[Statements|Statement]] is to ask for an interpretation of events that is in line with the [[Lines]] of a given [[Narratives|Narrative]]. There are, at times, conflicts between two understandings of magic; two [[Actors]] may have different views about a given subject, with one insisting their magic functions in a way that would be impossible in the second Actor's version of magic. [[to resolve|To resolve]] this conflict could require [[Conflicts|Conflict]] or [[Arbitration]]; however, savvy [[Players]] may instead choose to introduce a [[Threads|Thread]] or an [[Alternatives|Alternative]] that explains how a Statement works for both Actors, satisfying the madness of both.

Wicking is ultimately temporary; it allows for a [[Results|Result]] to factor in two different logical systems without threatening either logical framework. Wicking is the equivalent of *claiming* that there is a logical explanation while not examining it further than needed for the example. Both parties now have a reasonable Alternative that seems sensible enough for the magic to function. Wicking is also a weakness, however; by wicking a Statement, you [[to introduce|introduce]] the (Wicked) [[Features|Feature]] and make it susceptible to [[to melt|melting]].
## Feature - (Wicked)
*see also*

When a [[Statements|Statement]] has been [[to denote|denoted]] with [[to wick|(Wicked)]], it's probably been wicked over the course of [[Conflicts|Conflict]]. Now that it exists as a bridge between two [[Lines]], it links them together and produces a fragile Statement that is highly susceptible to [[to melt|melting]], [[to snuff|snuffing]], and [[to burn|burning]]. Care should always be taken to [[to denote|denote]] a Statement was wicked during [[Arbitration]].
