*Laconic: A player's window to respond to a director declaring a statement is fact.*

The **response window** is a five-to-ten second window under which a [[to challenge|challenge]] can be made to a [[Statements|Statement]] about to be [[to canonize|canonized]]. The Neverlands System is designed to be quick and snappy; if the [[Directors]] needed to wait for [[Players]] before taking any action, it could be disruptive to play. Directors could be more lenient and allow Players additional time, especially if they're new to the [[Neverlands System]], but eventually the Directors will need to take and complete actions to advance the [[Statuses|Game-State]].

Directors who take an action should give a brief pause; if a Player raises their hand, or asks for the Director to wait, the Director should listen. Five to ten seconds is generous; Players are likely to respond before that time window is over, but if another Player cuts in with side chatter during the response window, the Directors should remain aware of nonverbal cues from their Players. Shy Players may need additional hand-holding until they can build the confidence to respond quickly and efficiently, especially over Players 

On the other hand, this rule could be used to deter Players from attempting to disable or prevent other Players from acting. Asking for retroactive challenges may not be intentional or malicious, but the effects would be felt either way. Directors can simply tell a Player who acts outside the response window that they need to respond in a reasonable time-frame; as long as the Director gave adequate pause, the Statement will remain [[to canonize|canonized]].
## Note: Discomfort and the Response Window
Remember that Statements which make the Table [[Discomfort|uncomfortable]] should still be examined and potentially removed. When a Player feels uncomfortable, they might be unable to voice their thoughts until well outside the response window; asking to challenge a Statement because it makes *them* uncomfortable should be possible even when the response window has closed.
