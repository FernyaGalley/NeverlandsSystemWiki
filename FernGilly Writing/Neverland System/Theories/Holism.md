Laconic: to consider the whole to be more than the parts.

**Holism** is a curious concept sometimes employed during [[Arbitration]] and often employed when [[to cast|casting]] [[Cues]]; the Holist philosophy is to consider the whole of something as more than the sum of its parts. It is often [[Appropriateness and Relevance|appropriate]] to respond to a Prompt by indicating you are using a holistic method of spell-casting. You do not have to define what your Cues [[to target|target]] *exactly*, especially when defining a spell would violate [[Suspension of Disbelief|suspension of disbelief]].

Consider a bird; a bird contains a beak, talons, eyes, meat and feathers! But when it comes time to cast a spell to effect a 'bird', to what part of the bird does the spell apply? Does the spell apply only to the beak, or the heart, or does it apply to each feather individually? Obviously not. It's a spell for a *bird.* You didn't cast a spell targeting *feathers*, you cast the spell to effect the *bird*. 

Even if you did cast a spell to target the *feathers*, feathers are themselves holistic! Feathers have a vane, a shaft, barbs, an afterfeather and a quill. Barbs are holistic as well; barbs could contain many interlocking barbules, including a distal barbule, the ramus and the proximal barbule. Distal barbules are holistic; they're made with skin cells and bio-pigments. One of many bio-pigments found in birds is melanin; melanin is holistic, being a compound composed of oxygen, carbon, hydrogen and nitrogen. Oxygen is holistic; it is composed of electrons, protons and neutrons... and would you believe it, protons are also holistic! It's wholes all the way down!

Rather than engage in silly shenanigans every time someone casts a spell, we encourage [[Players]] to be [[Rule of Charitability]] and assume [[Strikes]] and Cues are applied in the most appropriate way to achieve the [[Casters|Caster's]] desired effect.
