*Tenses (v): gaslit (past), gaslight (present), gaslighting (progressive)*
*Laconic: To make someone believe they are insane.*

To **gaslight** an [[Actors|Actor]] is to tell them lies to make them doubt their own story. Gaslighting should *never* be done against [[Players]]; Players are intended to have all observable knowledge of the plot at all times. Players may choose not [[to observe]] a [[Facts|Fact]], and they may choose [[to believe]] in [[Fictions|Fiction]] or [[Opinions]], but the [[Tables|Table]] should avoid misleading one another. In [[Conflicts|Conflict]], however, gaslighting is encouraged for sneaky and manipulative [[Actors]].

To gaslight another Actor, simply rebut a [[Statements|Statement]] in play with a simple 'I don't believe that's what happened.' Playing this [[Opinions|Opinion]] is hard to defend against; the other Actors who witnessed the event will need to produce testimony [[to prove]] that the Actor who holds that Opinion lacks credibility, or [[to reveal]] evidence which shows the event happened as described. 

Gaslighting is an affirmative defense against most Statements. If the produced Opinion is [[to canonize|canonized]], it will [[to snuff|snuff]] any [[Links]] on the [[Racks|Rack]]. The only way to avoid getting snuffed after being gaslit is to play to the [[Sinks|Sink]] instead!
## Feature - (Gaslit)
*see also*

When a [[Statements|Statement]] has been [[to introduce|introduced]] with **(Gaslit)**, it and all links are effectively [[to snuff|snuffed]] until more evidence has been [[to reveal|revealed]]. Alternatively, testimony may be used [[to mitigate]] (Gaslit) by showing the [[Opinions]] of other [[Actors]] to be dubious evidence against your claim.
