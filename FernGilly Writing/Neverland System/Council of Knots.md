The **Council of Knots** is a [[Cults|Cult]] dedicated to the death of the [[Narrative Awareness System|Goddess of Suffering, N'as]]. The Council believes that by killing the Goddess, they can end the [[Cycle of Suffering]], the [[Constellations]] will be free and [[Mortals]] will all be able [[to cast]] [[Cues]]. They believe that the Goddess oppresses the free will of Mortals, and that it is the ultimate right of Mortals to express their free will at any expense; they will murder, rob, mangle and jaywalk in their pursuit of their agenda.

This is not to say that the Council is uncaring; independent Councilors are likely to be friendly and personable, and in general the Council will aid the [[Tables|Table]] in uprooting [[Fascists|Fascist]] Cults. They care deeply about their cause and will always side with the oppressed over the oppressors.

With that said, many Councilors are themselves selfish and aggressive; they will happily abuse the fear and kindness of others to get their foot in the door. Councilors are known to act as terrorists, setting fires to buildings and planting bombs in populated areas. They don't respect the personal space of others and assume that they are the 'main character' at all times; if their personal Cult is not deathly afraid of them, they must be new Councilors.

The head Councilor is known as the **Enemy of the World**. The Enemy of the World is an inherited position; when the previous Enemy of the World is bested, a new Councilor may take the mantle. [[Mysten]] follows the Enemy of the World religiously, as if they had a pact together; [[Seprix]] also takes special interests in the activities of the Enemy of the World. The Enemy of the World is, perhaps, the only person in the galaxy capable of threatening N'as and ending her eternal life once and for all.

Nobody has been successful. Yet. But certainly, the Council of Knots will try...

# Assets
Councilors have few [[Assets]] as a rule; they travel light by necessity. Most Councilors are fugitives and terrorists, so they're unlikely to be able to stay in [[Dragon-Temples]] for long. Councilors usually carry around their [[Sanctuaries|Sanctuary]] in their [[Pockets]]-- they live inside their favored mode of transport, never staying in one place for long unless they've found an interesting scheme to play out.

Councilors are expected to provide [[Rule of Economies|affordable]] services to [[Mages]] and Mortals who seek them out; they are expected to grant the *wishes* of the foolish and the damned, though their [[Blessings]] are [[Risk and Duress|risky]] and likely to backfire. It is a good policy to expect a Councilor to grant a wish for 'free', only for the price to be something the [[Actors|Actor]] was never going to be willing to pay. The Council of Knots will always provide advice and services to the accursed, helping them adapt to their new lifestyle, though that advice may simply be nagging and philosophy at best.
# Territory
The Council of Knots don't hold any Dragon-Temples, but they are talented at securing abandoned buildings and embedding themselves in a community without being noticed. Some Councilors are known to brazenly [[to manipulate|manipulate]] the minds of Mortals, turning them into obedient thralls. Most thralls are or were Fascists, so the Council turns a blind eye to these activities; still, Councilors who engage in acts of mental enslavement are likely to be punished and reproached by other Councilors once made aware.
