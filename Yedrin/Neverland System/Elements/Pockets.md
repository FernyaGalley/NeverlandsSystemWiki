Laconic: Elements are organized into Pockets for storage and play.

**Pockets** are [[Mechanics|mechanical]] [[Game Mechanics|Elements]] that represent empty spaces and ambiguous Elements. Any [[Game Mechanics|Element]] or [[Statements|Statement]] could fit into a Pocket that was of an appropriate shape and size; [[Costumes]] could fit into closets, and [[Typewriters]] could fit into a violin case. [[Cuffs]] might be stored in dresser drawers, and there would be no problem fitting [[Tomes]] inside of backpacks! But you could also take a Tome out of your bag and put [[Spools]] into your bag instead; we could hardly call the contents of the bag *fixed*.

Ambiguous [[Props]] like the ones found in a bag could be anything, so the space an ambiguous Prop *could* occupy is called a Pocket! [[to clarify|To clarify]] that ambiguity, [[Notes]] [[to denote|denoting]] what the Pocket contains should be present on any given Pocket. When an [[Actor]] [[to pocket|pockets]] a Prop, they are mechanically denoting the contents of the Pocket using the Elements they are pocketing as Notes. For more information, consult the [[Cues|Cue]] [[to pocket]].

Empty spaces built [[to play]] Statements to can also be Pockets. There could theoretically be a Statement inside a Tome; you wouldn't know if the Tome was blank or not until you opened the Tome to read its contents. Since the Tome *could* contain Statements, if you opened it and found nothing, you could write a Statement in that Tome; likewise, with a writing instrument like an eraser you could wipe away and existing Statement and replace it with your own. This empty space is an ambiguous space, and is therefore a Pocket! For more information on this interaction, consult the [[Theory of Ambiguity]].
