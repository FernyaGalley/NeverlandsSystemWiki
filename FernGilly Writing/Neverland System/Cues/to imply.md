*Tenses (v): implied (past), imply (present), implying (progressive)*
*Laconic: To remind others of what already exists.*

To **imply** a [[Statements|Statement]] or [[Features|Feature]] is present is to suggest that it has always been present, even if improperly [[to denote|denoted]]. This is different from [[to gaslight|gaslighting]] in that the [[Casters|Caster]] could make a logically [[to validate|valid]] argument for the presence of the implied [[Targets|Target]]; assuming [[Rule of Charitability|charitability]], the subject of the implication was likely not made evident out of haste, not malice.

It would certainly slow down the pace of the game if each Statement needed to be critically analyzed and broken down into tokenized sub-Statements and sub-Features; some amount of implication is necessary to keep [[Suspension of Disbelief]] and the [[Theory of Fun]] alive. 

With that being said, Directors are encouraged [[to obfuscate]] mysteries and use implication as a *technique* to avoid [[to foreshadow|foreshadowing]] their mysteries too early. Directors do not *need* to tell the [[Tables|Table]] that the vagrant they've been traveling with is secretly the prince of an ancient kingdom; it should be possible to *imply* as such without stating the truth outright. When the Table [[to present|presents]] a logically valid argument, [[Directors]] are **strongly encouraged** by the [[Theory of Honesty]] [[to reveal]] the truth.
## Feature - (Implied)
*see also*

When a [[Features|Feature]] or [[Statements|Statement]] is *(Implied),* that means the Statement or Feature is present even if the Table has not yet [[to denote|denoted]] its presence. It is likely [[Rule of Charitability|common sense]] that this critical and [[Appropriateness and Relevance|relevant]] [[Game Mechanics|mechanic]] is present even if it has not been denoted; if it seems easier to denote the (Implied) Feature or Statement from this point onward, [[Directors]] are encouraged to do so!
