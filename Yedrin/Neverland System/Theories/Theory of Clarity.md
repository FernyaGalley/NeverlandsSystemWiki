There are at times *duplicate* or *redundant* terms for the same concept in the [[Neverlands System]]. This is not an attempt to pad the system or confuse the [[Tables|Table]] with additional vocabulary to memorize; this is a deliberate attempt on the part of the System to make some vocabulary *easier* to memorize, especially when these terms are used adjacent in text. Consider the following Statement:

'The [[Props|Prop]] [[to cast|cast]] a [[Cues|Cue]] [[to pocket]] the Prop [[to relocate]] from the [[Positions|Position]] across a [[Threads|Thread]] to relocate to the next Position so the Prop could cast a Cue to pocket the Prop onto the new Position.'

This is a [[to validate|valid]] Statement in the Neverlands System, and there's no problem parsing this if you go slowly. Someone picked something up, carried it across the room, and set that something down. But it's confusing to read; it's a word teaser. What if, instead, we used a few *clarifying terms?*

'The [[Actors|Actor]] [[to pocket|pocketed]] a [[Props|Prop]], [[to relocate|relocated]] to [[to target|that]] [[Positions|Position]], and [[to drop|dropped]] that Prop.'

Much easier to read! Sometimes, clarifying terms are employed when we've determined a term is too *saturated* in the System, such as how the term Position is sometime used instead of Statement. For these clarifying terms, we try to make sure that the use case for the new term is fairly consistent.

Sometimes, clarifying terms are employed when a series of actions can be *implied* rather than executed step-by-step. Crossing a Prop means to relocate it between two or more Positions; it's easier to say 'cross' than describe the physical, redundant process of relocating something twice or more.

Lastly, clarifying terms are used when an action can have two different meanings. [[Players]] might not know you can pocket something to drop it, so the 'drop' Cue exists so players can intuitively guess their next Cue.
# Laconic Forms

Sometimes, when reading an article one might stumble across a Laconic [[Theory of Clarity|form]] provided for that article; this is a **clarifying term** designed to help [[Directors]] who are more informed quickly convey a mechanic to less informed [[Players]]!
