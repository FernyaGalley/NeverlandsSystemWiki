Laconic: Mortals with strong emotions and magic.

[[Mortals]] are fragile things; they live and wallow in suffering their whole lives. Some accept their lots in life; they are sadists who inflict suffering on others, or cogs to turn the wheel of suffering. These Mortals do not appreciate life; they cannot experience artistic beauty because they do not understand what it means to feel pain and grief. They look at their world as a commodity, not an ecosystem of which they are but one part.

A few Mortals cannot handle the pressures of modern society, and they simply break. These are the Mages; the ones who bear suffering on their shoulders, the weight enough to snap their spine. Mages are worn down enough their mind opens up to the *possibility* of magic, as they have been forcibly torn from all other avenues of escape. They embrace fantasy as a way to handle their crippling suffering, and they instinctively [[to wick|wick]] reality.

A Mage can *intentionally* [[to cast|cast]] [[Cues]]. This might seem obvious, but Mortals are not able to cast Cues on their own. From the Mortal's perspective, they are simply living their lives without knowledge of the Rack and Canon; but from a Mage's perspective, each action they take is *deliberate*, a spell that is cast through complex rites and rituals. Each conversation is an opportunity to make [[Statements]], each [[Conflicts|Conflict]] possible [[to resolve]] through lateral thinking and deep understanding of [[Alternatives]]. The world a Mage [[to observe|observes]] is not the world a Mortal *sees*, and Mages are unable to close their eyes once they have been opened.

Mages will naturally come to understand their [[Courts|Court]] and [[Suits|Suit]] as they become more familiar with the ways of magic. Most Mages are content once they've learned their [[Blessings|Blessing]]; that connection to a higher being can soothe the soul of a Mage enough to ease their pain. The understanding that life will continue after death, that there is a [[Narrative Awareness System|Goddess]] who watches over them; that's enough for many.
