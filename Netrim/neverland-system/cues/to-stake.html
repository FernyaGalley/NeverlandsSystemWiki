to stake

Tenses (v): staked (past), stake (present), staking (progressive)
Laconic: To raise the stakes of Conflict.

Lore

To stake an Element is to offer it and raise the stakes of Conflicts. This is a Cue which requires consent from the other Players at the Table, but it is an effective way to subvert an Apocalypse; by staking, an Actor effectively offers an all-or-nothing gamble to the other Cult.

Pinning Actors while the stakes are raised will result in catastrophic losses for their Cult, so Actors are often filled with determination they might not usually have. This is almost certainly the climax of the Act; remember that any staked Elements will be divided among all remaining Cults in Conflict equitably.

Mechanics of Staking

Mechanically, targeted Elements are introduced with (Staked) and every (Enjoined) Cult in Conflict is required to stake Assets of similar collective value to the (Targeted) Elements. The Directors weigh each offering, and if the stakes seem reasonable and the Table consents, the staked Assets are also introduced with (Staked).

Feature - (Staked)

see also Assets and Dances

When (Staked) is introduced to a Statement or Prop, then those Elements are now up for grabs in the current Conflict. If any Cult no longer has Actors in the Conflict, they lose all claims to everything which is (Raised); the remaining Cults will secure their Assets equitably.

Mechanics of (Staked)

Mechanically, (Staked) Assets in Canon snuff any attempts to pin Cultists that are part of the Cult which currently possesses said Assets. When Conflict ends, all (Staked) Assets and Elements are distributed equitably among the Cults left standing; the Directors arbitrate the Results.
