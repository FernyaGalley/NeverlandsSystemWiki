When [[Arbitration]] is performed **under duress**, that means that [[Cues]] are at greater **risk** of failure than normal. Cues performed under duress are automatically [[to snuff|snuffed]] unless a [[Players|Player]] or [[Actors|Actor]] takes precautions to [[to mitigate]] the risks.

What follows is a non-exhaustive list of [[Statuses]] under which a Cue is **risky** and one potential mitigation method. There are no shortage of unique cases or solutions that the we couldn't possibly imagine ourselves; risky situations not listed here is left as an exercise for the reader.

* Darkness could impede your tasks; a flashlight could beat away the darkness.
* Long hauls with heavy gear could leave you breathless; a short rest could help with fatigue.
* A carriage without fuel to operate may sputter and come to a halt; refueling could help.
* Carriages that run too long in the desert may overheat; coolant could keep the engine cool.
* Dashing across the room is only risky if you're out of shape. Stretch and exercise regularly!
* Addiction and alcoholism could cause internal conflict. Giving into alcoholism *might* mitigate the alcoholism, but it could also impair your judgement and make further actions harder.
* A problem gambler wouldn't have a problem if they left their wallet at home.
* Blurry vision is only an issue until you find and wear your glasses.
* It's easy to make a masterpiece in a studio designed to produce masterpieces. Performing that same action in a dingy alley surrounded by trash and scrap metal is another matter.
* Sparring with your master would be difficult and risky, but asking them to slow down and explain their techniques could help your comprehension greatly.
* Haste makes waste. Performing actions quickly is sure to make mistakes more common; slowing down would help, but it could also be a luxury you couldn't afford.
* Everything is riding on your success? That's a lot of pressure for one person to shoulder. Thankfully, you have your friends here to support you in your time of need!
* Doing the job of three men is difficult without three men to help you do the job!
* You've never fixed your own plumbing before? Thankfully you've got a toolbox and tutorials on the internet; imagine having to figure out plumbing from scratch!
* Building furniture is hard, but thankfully yours came with easy to follow blueprints. Right?
* It's hard to defend yourself in court; there's nothing wrong with calling on a professional.
* Making delicious food without a sense of taste is hard. Asking for a sous-chef sounds smart.
* Firing a rocket from a swerving car is sure to make a mess. Good thing you've got a laser sight and plenty of experience with rocketry. Down the helicopter goes!
* Keeping balance on a slippery surface can take all your focus, until you've put on the harness.
* It's a firefight? Good thing you found some durable cover. Concrete to the rescue!
* World's too loud for you? Pop on a pair of headphones and put on your favorite song.
* You have no idea what's going on, but listening to the brains of the crew has never steered you wrong. Do what they say and you don't *need* to know anything about the situation.
* This quiz is too hard-- thankfully you've smuggled in the answer sheet inside your pencil.
* Your friend likes the bottle a little *too* much. Out the window it goes-- no addiction risk now!
* This beautiful fae has you in quite a bind; what if you convince them to loosen their tail a bit?
* Fascist propaganda making your friend feel nervous? Tear it down! Out of sight, out of mind.
* So you've brought a knife to a gun fight? They've brought a gun to a knife fight-- close the distance and you're just as lethal as they were, if not more.
