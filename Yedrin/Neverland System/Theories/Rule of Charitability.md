The **Rule of Charitability** is a [[corollary]] [[Game Mechanics|Rule]] to the [[Neverlands System]]: *always assume mistakes are made out of haste, not malice*. 

What this means in practice is that there is a brief [[response window]] after any [[Statements|Statement]] is made that allows [[Players]] to 'take back' a previous Statement. Players may, in the heat of a moment, poorly phrase their Statement or leave a typo in that distorts the meaning of the Statement; they may [[to signal|signal]] to the other Players that they would like [[to melt]] their own Statement. As charitable and logical Players, [[Tables]] *ought* to give Players some amount of charitability for their actions and allow for the fix to go through, and [[Directors]] *ought* [[to close]] the [[Conflict State Handbook|Conflict State]] if necessary.

After a Player was given sufficient time to read their own Statement, and to make their fix, the Director should *open* the Conflict State and prevent further alteration; Players were given time [[to respond]] to their own Statements, so they should not be able to correct those Statements later! If a Player realizes they've made a grave mistake given new context *minutes* later, they may be out of luck; nothing short of [[Discomfort]] or [[extraordinary circumstances]] should allow them to melt that Statement now.
