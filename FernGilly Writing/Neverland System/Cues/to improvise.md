*Tenses (v): improvised (past), improvise (present), improvising (progressive)*
*Laconic: To make changes to the mechanics for any reason.*

To **improvise** a [[Scenes|Scene]] is to discard existing [[Game Mechanics]] in favor of different rules or mechanics. Sometimes, the [[Rules as Written]] are too restrictive to apply; generally speaking, if there are no [[Risk and Duress|risky actions]] taking place, mechanics are not necessary. Improvising is taking a step beyond that; the [[Directors]] can invent their own rules for [[Arbitration]] on the spot if it would be more [[Appropriateness and Relevance|reasonable]] than following the rules.

For example, a Director might bring out figurines to help them visualize the Scene for other [[Players]]. They might [[to resolve|resolve]] [[Statements]] on the [[Racks|Rack]] using the figurines; they could argue an [[Actors|Actor]] was too far away from a downed foe [[to pocket]] their weapon, or that their line of sight prevented them from [[to observe|observing]] a stealthy foe. These tools are not included in the Neverlands System, but we acknowledge they are genuinely useful for some cases and encourage Directors to look for ways to increase immersion.

Lastly, any use of improvisation should follow the [[Rule of Consistency]]. It is important that Players are able to predict how [[Cues]] will play out in advance.a
## Feature - (Improvised)
*see also*

When an [[Game Mechanics|Element]] is (Improvised), it was introduced by the [[Tables|Table]] using [[Game Mechanics]] not present in the [[Neverlands System]]. (Improvised) Elements should be carefully scrutinized by [[Directors]] during [[Arbitration]] to ensure they follow the [[Rule of Consistency]].
