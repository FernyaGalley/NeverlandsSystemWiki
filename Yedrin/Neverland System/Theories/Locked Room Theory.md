**[[Discomfort]] warning; suicide and self-harm are briefly discussed.**

**Locked room mysteries** do not, in actuality, exist. The door can be locked, certainly; if it were not guarded by a locked door, then the premise would not be so intriguing. But a locked room? Such a thing has never existed. For a crime to take place in a locked room, there are three possibilities:

* The crime is inflicted by someone in the room who never leaves the room.
* The incident never occurred in the first place.
* The room is not locked.

Examining the first premise; self-harm is not a *mystery* to be solved. If an [[Actors|Actor]] were to do grievous self-harm to themselves in a locked room, then the Actor is exercising their bodily autonomy. This is a very real possibility, but it is the last possibility; to avoid triggering the [[Discomfort|Rule of Discomfort]] we [[to encourage|encourage]] [[Directors]] to avoid using this premise in mysteries.

That does, however, leave the possibility of a second culprit inside the locked room. Since there is no 'lock' preventing a second culprit from crawling out from underneath a desk, killing the victim and hiding underneath the desk again, out of sight of the investigators, there's no 'locked room' in this mystery at all, only a door that is locked from a single perspective.

Then, we have the crimes that did not happen; the incidents which are imagined to be locked rooms, but are not locked rooms (or perhaps even incidents) in the first place. The corpse may have been placed in a locked room, then the incident made to look as if it was locked; you have no guarantee the crime took place inside the room. It is a possibility no crime took place at all, and the incident inside the locked room is imagined! If a victim died of coronary heart failure, we can hardly call that a murder.

Next, we look at the unlocked locked room; a room with multiple passages in or out. If there is a window that can be opened, then it is not a sealed room; anyone could simply walk in or out of the room, opening the possibility of a second culprit; since a second culprit could exist, the 'mystery' of the locked room is solved. There are no locks, and it only appeared to be locked from the first perspective.
