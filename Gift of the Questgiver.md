OUTLINE
Carly looks out the window. It's raining.
She looks down at the knotted rope in her hands.
Our protagonist (played by whom? call her Carly for now) tries to kill themselves.
Suicide by hanging.
They fumble the noose on the fan, break the fan, slip on the chair and break that too.
They're miserable and in pain but alive.
Breaking down and sobbing. They can't do anything right.
Looking up at the ceiling, she wishes for a way out.
There's a click at the window.
In horror, Carly glances at her bedroom window.
It's the second floor. Who could...?
The window is pitch black. No visibility of the sky.
The window forces itself open, inch by inch.
Fingers wrap around the frame.
A figure gradually pushes her way in.
She's tall, and slender. Her face is long, and her eyes shut tight.
Her smile reaches from ear to ear, and crystalline wings sit behind her.
Her long and crooked fingers interlock in prayer, and she tilts her head.
"Child," the voice asks. "What ails you so?"
Carly, understandably freaking out, tries to back up.
What in the rings of hell is that thing?
"I'm your guardian angel," the creature replies simply.
"You may call me Mysten. I saw you were in danger, and came to rescue..."
"Ah." The guardian angel looks down at the rope. She pieces the story together.
"I see. So the danger comes from within."
Carly, understandably horrified, is unable to respond.
Mysten proposes a solution.
"If the danger is from within, then..."
"We'll just have to cut it out of you, child."
Mysten reveals a simple kitchen knife in her long and slender fingers.
"Go ahead, child. Partake of my fruits."
"Th-that's... a knife." Carly is nervous about taking the blade.
"What's wrong with that?" Mysten wonders in her alien way.
"You want me to cut myself? Aren't you a guardian angel?"
"Sometimes things need to be cut to encourage healthy growth."
"Think of the human host as... a bonsai tree."
bonsai?
"One little cut. And it hurts. But the result grows back... much stronger."
"I... I don't think this is a solution," Carly stammers.
"Oh? And killing yourself is?" Mysten leans in close...
Mysten finally opens her eyes. Black voids where irises should be. Sunken eye sockets.
"I-I learned my lesson, ma'am," Carly whimpered.
The fear of god has burned the lesson into her brain.
Mysten hesitates.
"Are you suuure, Carly?" Mysten asks... to be sure.
Carly trembles under the creature's gaze.
She nods.
"Well! Glad that's resolved!" Mysten clasps her hands shut.
"I would hate to have had to cut all the bad parts out..."
"M-me too," Carly whimpered.
"You can keep the knife. As a reminder," Mysten emphasized.
"I'm going to go away now. There are lots of boys and girls who need my protection..."
Carly trembled, and then Mysten gave her a _big_ hug. She drops the knife.
"Stay healthy, Carly. I wouldn't want to have to visit a second time."
Mysten pulled away with her friendly warning, and stepped out the window... 
slowly closing it behind her.
Carly is left trembling.
Mysten walked out the second story window.

CH 2
Mysten offers another knife.
This time to deal with an animal that's annoying them.
Mysten is confused. Isn't this animal bothering them?
Why won't they use the knife?
Oh. They're not in danger?
Don't worry about that. Just hold onto the knife.
Carly still fears what Mysten will do if she doesn't have the knife.
The next day, the dog gets loose from the fence.
Mysten watches as Carly tosses the knife.
She doesn't kill the dog, just lets the dog bite her.
Mysten is disappointed.
She peeks from behind the curtain at the hospital.
Mysten desired a sacrifice, and Carly delivered nothing.
Carly said she couldn't bear to hurt an innocent being.
Mysten is so proud.
...outwardly proud. Carly passed her test.
She gives Carly the knife again... as a gift.

CH 3
Carly is roughly escorted from the hospital.
She's told not to bring a knife into the hospital.
Carly doesn't get her knife back.
She's glad. She didn't need the knife.
But Mysten is there. She smiles.
"Don't worry, Carly," Mysten promises.
"I got your knife for you."
Mysten presents the knife to Carly.
Carly doesn't grasp it. She's horrified.
Mysten simply holds the knife until Carly grasps it again.
The knife looks different. Carly swallows.
"This isn't my knife." It's not a kitchen knife.
"I thought you could do with something better," Mysten promised.
"You've proved you're responsible with a blade."
"I don't need a knife..." Carly whimpered.
"Of course you do," Mysten insisted. "How else will you defend yourself?"
"What... what if I hurt myself with it?" Carly looks for excuses.
Mysten opened her eyes, wide and bloodshot. She stared.
"...of course you wouldn't do that," Mysten insisted.
"R-right. Of course I wouldn't do that," Carly agreed.
Carly was scared.
Carly walked herself home, knife in hand.
Mysten followed behind her. Quiet. Eyes open.

CH 4
Carly was taking a long time to walk home.
She was still wearing a hospital gown...
Still wielding a bowie knife. She was in shoes, but not socks.
Mysten was still behind. Quiet. Listening.
She looked like a cat, the way she followed behind Carly.
Carly was openly avoided by others on the street.
She wants to drop the knife... but Mysten is right there.
Mysten doesn't say she has to hold the knife, but...
Carly knows better. Mysten doesn't have to say it.
Eventually, a cop car arrives. Two officers step out.
They approach Carly. One wears a mask, a fat and chubby man behind.
One of the brownshirt goons that got folded into the local militia.
The other isn't white, looks older. Likely a cop before President Armand took over.
both have guns on their holsters, in reach but not drawn.
Rare that officers show caution like this.
They keep their distance.
"Ma'am," one of the officers speaks. "We've gotten reports about..."
"A crazy woman escaped from a mental institution wielding a machete."
"...I'm not crazy," Carly stammered, openly trying to forget about the angel behind her.
"And I'm not saying that's you... but I am asking you to look at the appearance, ma'am."
He's afraid of the knife. Afraid of the power Carly holds.
Carly looks down at her gown. At her knife.
"I... yes, it looks bad," Carly agreed.
"Can you put the knife down, then?" the cop asked. "We can talk if you put it away."
The officer offered her a way out. She didn't take it... couldn't take it.
Carly was paralyzed with fear looking behind the officer.
Mysten slowly reached around the fat officer behind him. She peeled back the mask.
Mysten slowly replaced the mask. The brownshirt didn't move.
Mysten slinked back into the shadows.
"Come on, Joe," the fat brownshirt snorted. "Why are we entertaining this?"
"No cameras, no witnesses. This would be real quick... no paperwork."
"We're cops, man," Joe hissed back. "We're social workers, not murderers."
"It's called 'de-escalation.' A tactic your fat keister should have learned in ICE."
Carly was terrified. Judas was threatening to murder her; no witnesses.
She couldn't drop the knife, either, because Mysten was watching.
Carly wasn't sure what her guardian angel was planning.
Wasn't a guardian angel supposed to keep you out of danger?
"Ma'am, please try to ignore him," Joe continued. "Set down the knife and..."
"We'll get you home after. Quick ride in the squad car and we'll be home."
Mysten curled her hands around Carly's shoulder now, leaning in close.
"The officer speaks true. He'll take you home, safe and sound."
"That one, though..." a crooked, long finger pointed at Judas.
"He is Judas. And tonight, he will break into your home. He will kill you."
"He will break into your home. He will tie you up. Judas will hurt you."
"He will put a bullet in your delicate skull... he will sleep with your corpse."
"Judas will be indicted by a federal prosecutor, for rape and murder."
"The lawyers will argue it was a 'defensive rape'. He will lose his case."
"He does not win on appeal; he does not need to win. That was never the game."
"After six months in prison, he is pardoned by the President of America."
"Your death remains unavenged," Mysten whispered. "But not this time."
"He is not innocent. There is no man free of sin, but especially not him..."
