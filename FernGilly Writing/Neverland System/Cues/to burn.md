*Tenses (v): burned (past), burn (present), burning (progressive)*
*Laconic: To ensure the success of something.*

To **burn** a [[Statements|Statement]] is to ensure that [[Arbitration]] will be unfairly biased towards that [[Statements|Statement]]. Whatever the [[Results|Results]] of Arbitration may be, improve them by one stage; a [[Dull]] Result becomes a [[Dim]] Result, and a [[Dim]] Result becomes a [[Bright]] result. If a Result is already Bright, the result remains Bright.

Even burning a Statement will not *magically* make it logically [[to validate|valid]]. If a burned Statement *cannot* be true because it violates [[Suspension of Disbelief]] or [[Discomfort]], [[Directors]] are encouraged to [[to melt|melt]] or [[to wick|wick]] the Statement into something *more* defensible, with the [[Tables|Table's]] consent. If the Table does not consent to melting an invalid Statement, it might not become [[to canonize|(Canonized)]].
## Feature - (Burned)
*see also [[to snuff|(Snuffed)]] (antonym), as well as its corollaries [[to wick|(Wicked)]] and [[to melt|(Melted)]]*

Whenever a [[Statements|Statement]] is burned, it is [[to introduce|introduced]] with (Burned); this [[Features|Feature]] ought to be [[to denote|denoted]] when introduced as it has a very important [[Appropriateness and Relevance|relevance]] to [[Arbitration]]. (Burned) Statements are especially *vulnerable* to both [[to wick|wicking]] and [[to melt|melting]].  A Statement which is both [[to snuff|(Snuffed)]] *and* (Burned) is neither; both Features are to be [[to estrange|estranged]] at the [[Directors|Director's]] earliest convenience, allowing the Statement to be [[to introduce|introduced]] to a second snuffing or burning.
