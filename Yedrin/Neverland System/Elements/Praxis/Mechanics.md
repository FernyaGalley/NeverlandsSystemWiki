**Mechanics** are the ***physical***  and ***mechanical*** forms of [[Praxis]] which produce [[Results]] from objective inputs. Mechanics are predictable actions, and the same inputs under the same conditions will always produce the same outputs without exception. They are the bedrock of the [[Neverlands System]], in that someone is not playing the Neverlands System if they are not using these Mechanics. 
# Identifying Mechanics

Mechanics are *typically* prefaced by the word **mechanically** in [[English]] [[Translations]] of the [[Neverlands System]]. When you see the phrase **Mechanically,** assume that everything which follows is a mechanic.

[[Cues]] can be identified by the presence of an **underlined verb**, such as [[to relocate]]. Cues may come in several verb forms, such as [[to relocate|relocated]] and [[to relocate|relocating]]; all variants should be listed in the Cue page.

[[Dynamics]] will always be followed by the suffix **-dynamic** and will never be plural, as seen in the term [[Maid-dynamic]]. If *only* a Dynamic is listed in text, as in the phrase [[Knight-dynamic]] [[Prop]], anything with that Dynamic is a valid [[Targets|Target]] for the Mechanic.

[[Features]] can be identified by their use of parentheses around the Feature, such as in [[to pin|(Pinned)]] and [[to canonize|(Canonized)]]. If it doesn't have parentheses, it's a Cue, not a Feature. If *only* a Feature is listed in text, as in the phrase [[to welcome|(Welcomed)]] [[Actors|Actor]], anything with that Feature is a valid Target for the Mechanic.

[[Pockets]] will likely be followed by the suffix **-pocket**, as seen in the term [[Witch-pockets]]. They may have nicknames in addition to their -pocket forms.

[[Statements]] *may* be followed by [[Values|Value]]-dynamic [[Notes]] such as [[Facts|(Fact)]], [[Fictions|(Fiction)]], [[Opinions|(Opinion)]] or [[Prompts|(Prompt)]], though only [[Fictions|(Fiction)]] is required due to the [[Theory of Honesty]]. Other Value-dynamics may be merely [[to imply|implied]] as part of the Statement.

[[Positions|Position]]-dynamic [[Notes]] are *always* [[to imply|implied]] and should only be [[to denote|denoted]] as necessary when mechanics require them to be [[to clarify|clarified]], otherwise your [[Conflicts]] will be much slower than intended.
# Understanding Mechanics

[[Mechanics]] have three outcomes depending on their odds of success; [[Bright]], [[Dim]] and [[Dull]]. We call these outcomes [[Results]], and there are four ways [[to dispute]] those Results; [[to melt|melting]], [[to wick|wicking]], [[to burn|burning]] and [[to snuff|snuffing]]. Think of a Mechanic like a candle; you can set the candle on fire and it will produce a light. You can let the candle melt and learn what it smells like and what it's made of; you can add fuel to a melted candle by adding a new wick to burn. Or, you could snuff the candle out, reducing its flame to nothing but smoke!
 
 The metaphor of 'Mechanics as candles' is used to make it easier to understand how Mechanics work for new [[Players]]. They should not be confused with the candles of a [[Candelabra]], which are [[Props]] with the ability [[to pocket]] Results; Candelabra follow mechanical rules and *are* Mechanics, but Mechanics are not Candelabra.
