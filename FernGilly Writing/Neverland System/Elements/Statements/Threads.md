*Laconic: Two or more Statements are linked by a different Statement.*

A **Thread** is a [[Major Dynamics|Major Dynamic]] of [[Positions]] that [[to signal|signals]] a link between other adjacent Positions. The following **facts** about Threads are true:

**Positions supported by a Thread are logically linked to other Positions.**
Threads act as transitions between two Positions. They allow Actors to easily [[to relocate|relocate]] along those Positions while still holding each Position, as the Positions are linked together and support one another. As long as the Thread is not [[to sever|severed]], the Positions are functionally identical.

**If an [[Actors|Actor]] [[to hold|holds]] a Thread, they hold all linked Positions as well.**
If a Position is true and [[to validate|valid]], and the other Position linked by a Thread is *also* true and valid, then by holding a Position on the Thread an Actor is necessarily assuming both Positions to be true and valid. 

**Threads may share the Major Dynamics of *all* linked Positions.**
When [[Appropriateness and Relevance|appropriate]], [[Directors]] should interpret Threads as linking two Positions together in such a way that a Thread seamlessly blends between two Positions. Threads must compensate for their connections if they do not want to gain the Major Dynamics of those Positions. When appropriate, it is [[to imply|implied]] that Threads share all Major Dynamics.

Example: A preface such as 'the smart-ass is right, we have to work together' may sufficiently [[to mitigate|mitigate]] a harsh Statement by acknowledging that a Position linked to this one is a [[Hazards|Hazard]], while also [[to imply|implying]] there is a [[to validate|valid]] point being made. If the Caster had neglected to call out the other Actor for their rude behavior, their new Thread would be a Hazard as well.

**Threads may at times share all other [[Game Mechanics|Elements]] of their linked Positions.**
As Threads exist to bridge two concepts together, they will take some of those concepts into themselves through their nature. Actors may freely move, for example, [[Props]] between two linked Positions thanks to a Thread ; while that Thread may not contain those Props as Threads lack [[Pockets]] unless [[to pocket|(Pocketed)]], it could allow the Actor to carry those Props across.
