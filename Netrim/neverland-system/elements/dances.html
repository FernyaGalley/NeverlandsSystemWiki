Dances

Laconic: Overturning the existing order.

As every Act of the Narrative opens, every major Cult in the Narrative must play a previously canonized Hill to Canon or a new Hill to the Outlines. After playing their Hill, if they wish, they may plot an Apocalypse. An Apocalypse overturns an existing Hill in Canon or on the Act Rack; if the Apocalypse remains uncontested by any other Cult by the end of the Act, the Hill it targets is permanently (Disputed) and the Cult which played the Apocalypse may canonize a new Hill for their own Cult.

Revolutionary Apocalypses

The Table always works towards a Revolutionary Apocalypse. The Table inevitably opens with the Revolutionary Apocalypse that establishes the Actors of the Table as a unified team; the first Act of a Narrative will always end with the Table's chosen actors unified towards a single cause, whatever that may be. It is a Fact that there is no force which can prevent the Table from plotting their first Apocalypse. The Table will always know who the heroes of the Narrative are by the end of the first Act and the causes each Actor stands for.

The Table may declare as many Revolutionary Apocalypses as there are Hills to die on; successfully disputing a Hill will let the Table play an additional Hill to the Outlines. Failure to complete any Revolutionary Apocalypse in the Act during which it was declared results in all Hills played to the Rack by the Table being snuffed during the final Arbitration of the Outlines.

Fascist Apocalypses

Inevitably, the Fascists will attempt to perform an Apocalypse wherever and whenever they wish to hold power. Fascists inevitably open the Narrative with one successful Apocalypse; no matter what the Players do, they will succeed at conquering enough territory to pose a credible threat to the Table as a whole. It is a Fact that the Table will know the identity of the (Figurehead) Fascist by the end of the first Act, and it is a Fact that the Fascists will hold enough power to both threaten and resist the Table by the end of the first Act. This is not to say that the Fascists are competent or even have a coherent plan; rather, their first Apocalypse will always succeed because the Narrative exists in response to the actions of the Fascists.

At the end of each Act in the Narrative, the Fascists will always either attempt to thwart a Revolutionary Apocalypse (if one exists) and start an Apocalypse of their own; even if another Cult starts their own Apocalypse, the Fascists will always take advantage of that Apocalypse to start a different Fascist Apocalypse. If there are multiple Fascist Cults, Directors are encouraged to have all Fascist Cults work together to conduct a single Apocalypse and interfere in every other Apocalypse rather than start multiple Apocalypses.

Brewing A Successful Apocalypse

To challenge an Apocalypse, a Cult must successfully fend off all challengers while maintaining their Hill(s). Whenever a Cultist enters an appropriate Conflict during an Apocalypse, their first Foundation must always be linked to one Hill by a Thread. Cultists must not be relocated from their Hill, pinned against their own Hill, or witness the Thread between the Hill and Foundation become severed during any Conflict relevant to the Apocalypse. If they meet any of these three conditions, that Cultist is pinned for all subsequent Conflicts.

When a sufficient number of Cultists are pinned, such that defending against the Apocalypse is no longer possible, the Director declares that the Apocalypse was successful and introduces (Disputed) on the opposing Hill. The Director may then allow the Cult which successfully completed their Apocalypse to introduce an appropriate Hill to the Act Rack; most often appropriate hills include freshly captured territory, turning a Cultist into a (Supporting) Actor for the Table or swaying the losing Cult to support the winning Cult's cause.

Dissolving a Cult in response to an Apocalypse is difficult as long as they maintain some semblance of power; Directors should exercise caution when deciding if a Cult should dissolve in the wake of an Apocalypse. If it would be more reasonable to dissolve the Cult's Assets and Cultists and reallocate the Cultists among the Fascists, Table and other interested Cults, the Director is encouraged to make those changes.

The Last Apocalypse

If a Fascist Cult no longer has any useful Hills to die on, then the (Figurehead) Fascist commits to the Last Apocalypse. The Last Apocalypse is a last-ditch effort to hold onto power; Fascists will break any and every principle of their moral code (which is saying a lot, as they have no morals) in order to hold onto power. They will acquire nuclear weapons, threaten to drive their ship into a black hole, infuse themselves with the tainted ichor of gods; they will toss away the entire potential of their movement to conduct one last awful spell to destroy everything.

It is a fact that if the Last Apocalypse succeeds, the Narrative is not yet over. There is always a chance to fight back, to try one more time to make the world a better place. There is one more fact to keep in mind; the Last Apocalypse is the moment in which the Fascist Cult is most vulnerable. If the Table can succeed at pushing back the Last Apocalypse, if every Cult can gather their power in one final push, the threat of Fascism can be eradicated and the Players can finally secure their hard-earned victory. Good luck.
