Praxis

Disambiguation: Elements, Mechanics, Notes, Theories and Poems are all forms of Praxis; Praxiology (the study of Praxis) also leads here.

Laconic: The actions Players take to play the Neverlands System.

Praxis is the process of changing Narratives to suit the needs of your Tables. Without input from the Players and Directors at the Table, Narratives are dead and lifeless. Narratives require interaction, and Praxis demonstrates the ways in which Players might interact with Narratives and one another.

The Five Forms of Praxis

Every process in the Neverlands System is a form of Praxis:

Elements are the metaphorical and meta-textual forms of Praxis. Elements are the building blocks of a Narrative; its Actors, the Stages they play upon, the Dynamics they share and the Scenes they play in. Each individual component of the Narrative is an Element. Elements are used to represent and reference other concepts in the Narrative for ease of participation.

Mechanics are the physical and mechanical forms of Praxis which produce Results from objective inputs. Mechanics have three outcomes depending on their success; Bright, Dim and Dull. Mechanics are predictable, and the same inputs will always produce the same outputs without exception. They are the bedrock of the Neverlands System and aid in Player understanding.

Notes are the notarial and administrative forms of Praxis. Notes are objective observations made by Tables with the intent to use them as guard rails for Narratives; they are predictable, and can each take the form of Facts, Fictions, Prompts and Opinions. As the bedrock that Narratives are built upon, Notes will always produce the same outputs without exception.

Theories are the social and ethical forms of Praxis. There are many ways to engage with a Narrative, but some forms of engagement are deleterious to social welfare and fun. Scribes encourage Players to read through the Theories behind the Neverlands System to build a mental framework of the Praxis this system embodies. By understanding Theories, one can better execute Praxis.

Poems are the narrative and dramatic forms of Praxis. These are the elements of the Neverlands System which are most open to interpretation, designed to make it easy to produce a Narrative which the Table can enjoy writing together. They are flavorful and full of allusions to literary and world history, but they are subjective; a Poem is easily misunderstood.

Directors and the Table are encouraged to write their own Poetry, especially about their own Actors and Constellations, but there is no shame in using what has also been provided.

Any Praxis could be a hybrid of multiple forms of Praxis; Features are Mechanics because they are used to create predictable Results, but they are also Notes because they could be used in Arbitration.

Execution of Praxis

Laconic: Executing Praxis faithfully is a fool's errand.

Scribes have discouraged Players from thinking of Praxis as a rigid practice. Instead, Players are instead encouraged to consult the Neverlands System as if it were a living document, a creature of paper and text that could be mistaken and may need to be amended in the future. Scribes have tried to make the Theories behind each decision the Neverland System makes as clear as possible, but as Scribes are Mortals they are not omniscient.

Each reader will come away with a different understanding of the Neverlands System, and each understanding will be further and further distorted from the deeply held Opinions of the Scribes; see the Theory of Authorial Intent for more information on this process. With this understanding in mind, the Scribes hope that whenever Players find Theories or Mechanics too burdensome or uncomfortable, they keep in mind the Theory of Fun.

Players are discouraged from spending their limited energies in a pursuit that does not bring them joy. With that said, Scribes hope that Players will find the Neverlands System both fun and memorable for years to come!
