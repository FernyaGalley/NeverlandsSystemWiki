The **Cycle of Suffering** is the process by which [[Mortals]] become [[Mages]], [[Echoes]] and [[Divines]]. It is a process which is mediated by the [[Narrative Awareness System|Goddess of Suffering, N'as]] and which has taken many forms over the centuries, with each [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] and [[Cults|Cult]] defining their own Cycle.

Firstly, it is a universal proposition that all [[Actors]] experience suffering. The amount of suffering Actors experience is not equal; some number of Actors suffer more than others. Actors who suffer the most fall below the second root of the variance between all suffering Actors; they fall outside the standard deviation. Actors who fall outside the range of standard deviation are eligible to become [[Mages]] without paying an additional price to do so.

**Mages** are capable of [[to cast|casting]] [[Cues]] and teach other Mortals to become Mages themselves. Among their number, a few appeal to the sensibilities of [[Neverlanders]]; these Mages are able to enter pacts with these ancient species and become Divines.

**Divines** are the combination of Neverlander(s) and a Mage; they are Mages with more magical and spiritual capabilities than their peers. It is rare, though not unheard of, for a Divine to be formed from someone who does not fall outside the standard deviation of suffering. The process of **Tempering** is painful; the body is purged of its pollutants and sins in an agonizing, hours-long process facilitated by the Goddess of Suffering. Once all impurities have been purged, the Divine must go through Tempering every so often to remove new impurities from their body; especially nasty Divines who live in modern society may have stronger reactions to Tempering than those who live ascetic and quiet lives.

Divines and Mages who pass away will form **Echoes**; these are Neverlanders who represent their essence and carry their memories into the future. Some Echoes choose to undergo **Reincarnation** and become Mortals again, following the plans of the God of Suffering; others choose to be guided by the [[Five-River Ferrymen]] to the [[Lunar Constellation]], where they live out their eternity in a run-down 'paradise'. When a Mortal drinks the blood of god, they may perish immediately and become an Echo; this process is called [[to syndicate|syndication]] and their victims are colloquially known as the [[Syndicated]].
