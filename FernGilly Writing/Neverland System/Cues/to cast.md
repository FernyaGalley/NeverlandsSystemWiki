*Tenses (v): (past), (present), (progressive)*
*Laconic: Using a Cue or Strike to accomplish a goal.*

To **cast** is to use a [[Cues|Cue]] or [[Strikes|Strike]] to gain a mechanical benefit. By playing a Cue or a Strike against a [[Targets|Target]] (likely followed by a [[Statements|Statement]]), a [[Players|Player]] may manipulate the [[Racks|Rack]] and [[Canons|Canon]].

Casting is different from making a Statement alone; casting implies the use of *[[Rule of Magical Thinking]]* and *[[abstraction]]*. A Statement which is the result of a cast can be easily [[to melt|melted]] and [[to wick|wicked]], and is much less likely to be logically [[to validate|(Valid)]] because it often lacks the [[Threads]] that would connect logical conclusions together. Casting accomplishes with magical brute force what a deft and charismatic hand could without relying on any other [[Game Mechanics|rules or mechanics]].
## Feature - (Cast)
*see also*

Whenever a Statement is produced by a Cue or a Strike, it is [[to imply|implied]] to also have the (Cast) [[Features|Feature]]. This Feature makes it more vulnerable to [[Arbitration]] as a rule, but is unlikely to be [[Appropriateness and Relevance|relevant]] to Arbitration in the course of play.

Clever [[Directors]] are encouraged to use the (Cast) Feature [[to denote]] that an Actor or Prop is the product of [[Rule of Magical Thinking]] or [[abstraction]] and that the Actor (or Prop) is a stand-in for something else. Magical illusions and allusions are easier [[to foreshadow]] if you keep their Features in mind.
