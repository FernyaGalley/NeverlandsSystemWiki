For those familiar with social cues in roleplay, this handbook may feel redundant at times. As a form of best practice, the [[Neverlands System]] never assumes familiarity with any subject, and each part of the book is designed for readers of all experience levels. When it comes to the **Conflict State**, Players and Directors will both need to intuit when the Conflict State is **open*** or **closed**. Conflicts are only expected to take between fifteen and thirty minutes each, so the more experienced Players are with social cues the smoother Conflict will beome.

In an **(Open) State**, we expect Players at the Table to be examining the Statements in play. (Open) State are rarely quiet-- they're filled with questions and banter, hands reaching in and out of the Rack and adding to its contents. Players need time to read, to think over their arguments and to make new Statements for the Rack. Directors need time to mull over the consequences of the actions players take and formulate their own arguments. We recommend Directors try to reach an (Open) State as often as possible, since an (Open) State gives both Players and Directors the freedom they need to make dramatic reveals and fancy strikes.

## Final Note
Neverlands System operates differently from other games in its genre. Typical games use a 'turn' format to determine who can act in an (Open) State. With each Player waiting around and doing nothing while combat proceeds, Players of these systems are intended to use that time to figure out their next move. We've found that giving Players real-world downtime tends to lead them to distraction and disinterest, and those two factors slow down the pace of the game dramatically. We intend for all Players to be fully engaged at all times during Conflict, and so we've designed the (Open) State to keep players on their toes.

Remember that the [[Order of Conflict Resolution]] suggests that Directors ask the Table if they need to grab snacks, food, or use the restroom before Conflict formally begins. Directors need about three to five minutes to [[to establish]] the Stage and Lines in [[Canons|Canon]], so Players are encouraged to use the (Closed) State that Conflict opens with to make refills or calls. Taking the time now to meet your physical needs will save the whole Table time later during conflict; Players should not expect to have a moment's rest during Conflict, as giving the other Players an extra ten minutes to examine the Statements in Conflict can lead to an extremely unfair advantage. Nobody wants to be caught flat-footed because they were in the restroom while someone discovered the true culprit of a murder mystery.
