*Laconic: Time spent relaxing between risky activities.*

**Intermissions** are time spent between stressful and [[Risk and Duress|risky]] activities. Intermissions are important; [[Mortals]] need Intermissions [[to slumber]], [[Mages]] need Intermissions to unravel, [[Divines]] need Intermissions to manage their [[Cults]] and [[Neverlanders]] need Intermissions to adopt new [[Courts]].

Intermissions may be performed within any [[Sanctuaries|Sanctuary]], so the rules of Sanctuaries still apply; [[to enjoin|enjoining]] [[Conflicts|Conflict]] is incredibly difficult [[to cast]] during Intermissions. If tensions are high for whatever reason, the [[Directors]] should [[to encourage|encourage]] [[Players]] to have their [[Actors]] bottle up their feelings, take whatever actions they need to reluctantly push through that Intermission and emotionally unload on each other when the next Conflict comes along.

During Intermissions, Cults may also manage their [[Assets]] and the [[Tables|Table]] may exchange [[Props]] and [[Dynamics]] that are sitting inside their [[Pockets]]; any Props or Dynamics not contained within a Pocket can be deposited with the other Assets belonging to the Cult or inside their Sanctuary, where they will likely remain safe and untouched by other parties.

[[to disrupt|To disrupt]] the Intermission of a Cult (or [[to pocket]] their Assets and forcibly [[to relocate|relocate]] into their Sanctuary, for that matter) is to plan an [[Dances|Apocalypse]]. The moment that the Apocalypse starts, the Intermission ends abruptly and most benefits of Intermissions are lost, depending on what the Directors find [[Appropriateness and Relevance|reasonable]]. The Table should be informed in advance that their Intermission will be interrupted so they may play accordingly.
