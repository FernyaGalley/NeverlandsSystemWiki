*Tenses (v): subverted (past), subvert (present), subverting (progressive)*
*Laconic: To stop an Apocalypse or undo an Accord.*
## Lore

To **subvert** an [[Dances|Apocalypse]] or [[Accords|Accord]] is to undermine the premise. To subvert an Accord, you need to make an Apocalypse happen; such a big change to the rules of a [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] will surely draw the attention and ire of those looking to maintain the status quo, especially [[Priestesses]] and [[Cultists]] who respect the desires of their [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]].

([[Fascists]] may also take issue with any attempts to change or improve life, though their reasons are much less comprehensible.)

Once an Apocalypse or Accord is subverted, the [[Tables|Table]] shouldn't have to worry about the opposing [[Cults|Cult]] trying their plan a second time; the Cult which tried [[to cast]] the Apocalypse will feel discouraged and return to the drawing board. There may be future attempts, but it is increasingly unlikely they will try again in the same way! Likewise, subverting an Accord will make it hard [[to consider]] a second attempt as Priestesses usually respect the will of their Dragon-Temple, even when Dragon-Temple makes unhealthy decisions.
## Mechanics of Subverting

**Mechanically,** [[to target|targeted]] [[Game Mechanics|Elements]] should be [[to consider|considered]]; if the [[Dances|Apocalypse]] [[to end|ends]] in a [[Bright]] [[Results|Result]], nothing happens. If the Apocalypse ends in a [[Dull]] Result, that Apocalypse (and any related [[Accords]]) should be [[to introduce|introduced]] with [[to subvert|(Subverted)]].

If the Apocalypse ends in a [[Dim]] Result, the [[Directors]] should carefully [[to consider|consider]] which parts of the Apocalypse were [[Appropriateness and Relevance|successful]] and which parts were not; they should rewrite the initial Apocalypse as different, smaller Apocalypses. Directors may introduce [[to canonize|(Canonized)]] to one set of Apocalypses and Accords, and [[to subvert|(Subverted)]] to the other Apocalypses and Accords.
## Feature - (Subverted)
*see also [[Accords]] and [[Dances]]*

When an [[Accords|Accord]] or [[Dances|Apocalypse]] is successfully introduced with **(Subverted)**, it has been undermined or altered to accomplish a different goal than intended. Much like a monkey's paw, when the Apocalypse finally [[to end|ends]], what the [[Cults|Cult]] gets is not what was intended; the rules of the game have changed.

An artifact intended to power a [[Fascists|Fascist]] death ray is instead subverted to power a Cult's [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]]; a Dragon-Temple's Accord against kissing is finally subverted as two athletes passionately embrace in full view of the city. Once [[to subvert|(Subverted)]], an Accord would be [[Appropriateness and Relevance|unreasonable]] to reinstate and it would take a miracle to organize that Apocalypse for a second time.
## Mechanics of (Subverted)

**Mechanically,** [[(Subverted)]] [[Game Mechanics|Elements]] are treated as [[to dispute|(Disputed)]] Elements for the rest of the [[Narratives|Narrative]]. The [[Directors]] may [[to consider|consider]] if it would be [[Appropriateness and Relevance|appropriate]] [[to mitigate]] [[to subvert|(Subverted)]] for special cases, or if enough time has passed and the [[Cults|Cult]] which sought the [[Dances|Apocalypse]] or [[Accords]] have a *new* plan. The new plan should be sufficiently unique from prior attempts!
