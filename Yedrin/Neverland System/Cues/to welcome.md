*Tenses (v): welcomed (past), welcome (present), welcoming (progressive)*
*Laconic: To have a Stage personally acknowledge your presence.* 

To **welcome** an [[Actors|Actor]] to a [[Stages|Stage]] is [[to reveal]] a number of [[to observe|(Observed)]] [[Statements]] about a Stage to the other [[Players]] at the [[Tables|Table]]. Players tend not to know much, if anything, about the setting in which the story takes place. It falls to the [[Rule of Qualifications|most qualified]] Player at the Table to describe a [[Stages|Stage]], effectively 'grounding' the Table by giving them a good idea of what their Actors are looking at in this very moment.

Typically, welcoming Actors to a Stage should be followed by describing the Stage's [[Accords]] to other Players and notifying the Table if there are any governing [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] ruling over the area; these are considerations that are important to the mechanics of some [[Cues]] and [[Positions]].

If the most qualified Player is ever unsure about whether or not something is [[(Present)]] or [[Appropriateness and Relevance|reasonable]], they should defer to the [[Directors]] for guidance.
## Feature - (Welcomed)
*see also*

When an [[Actors|Actor]] has been [[to introduce|introduced]] with **(Welcomed)**, they have been [[to accept|accepted]] by the local [[Dragon-Temples|Dragon-Temple]] as a guest or tourist and will be treated as such by local [[Cults]]. [[Cultists]] will know instinctively that an Actor is an outsider; for an outsider to blend in, they'll need [[to disguise]] themselves or participate in Cult activities. Cults are not required to treat their guests well; with that said, any [[Game Mechanics|Element]] participating in Cult activities is far more likely to be welcomed than hunted down and killed. Actors are encouraged to 'play along' and follow the [[Accords]] to maintain their (Welcomed) status.
